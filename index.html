Welcome to Santasa Counselling

“Santasa” means Joy — and that’s the heart of everything we do. At Santasa Counselling, we believe that true joy and balance arise from understanding ourselves and nurturing our relationships with empathy and openness. Life often brings moments that feel overwhelming, confusing, or emotionally draining — and during such times, having a safe, non-judgmental space to talk can make all the difference. Our sessions are non-clinical, focusing on emotional well-being, relationship harmony, and personal growth. Whether you’re navigating parenting challenges, relationship stress, life transitions, or academic pressures at school or college, we’re here to walk beside you — with warmth, respect, and compassion.

About the Counsellor

Keerthi holds a Bachelor’s Degree in Law (BA LLB), an Advanced Certification in Counselling, and a Diploma in Child Psychology. She is also a Certified Emotional Wellness Counsellor.

She works with individuals, families, and parent–child relationships to navigate emotions, build resilience, and create more balanced, fulfilling lives. Through practical tools and compassionate guidance, she supports emotional well-being in everyday life.

Her approach blends gentle, open conversation with effective techniques that help people reconnect, heal, and grow. Keerthi believes that real change begins when people feel truly seen and heard — not analysed or judged. Her sessions are collaborative, empathetic, and deeply human, focusing on everyday experiences rather than clinical treatment.

Our Vision & Approach

Our process is client-centred, built on the belief that every individual and family deserves a safe, non-judgmental space to share their thoughts and feelings openly. We use evidence-based techniques to help families, parents, and children enhance communication, build emotional resilience, and foster personal growth.

At Santasa, our work is grounded in empathy, respect, and personalised care. We tailor every session to meet each child’s and family’s unique needs, combining effective techniques with warmth and understanding.

Our techniques include Cognitive Behavioural Techniques, Solution-Focused Techniques, Art and Play-Based Techniques, Narrative Techniques, and Family-Centred Techniques, along with mindfulness techniques, emotional regulation techniques, and behavioural techniques.

At Santasa, we aim to empower individuals and families to strengthen their relationships, overcome challenges, and build a lasting foundation for emotional well-being.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services

Counselling is a professional and confidential process where a trained counsellor works with individuals, couples, or families to explore and address emotional and relational challenges. It offers a safe, non-judgmental space for people to openly share their thoughts, feelings, and concerns. Through active listening, empathy, and supportive techniques, counselling helps foster self-awareness, emotional healing, and personal growth.

Santasa Counselling offers an empathetic, non-judgmental approach led by a counsellor qualified in Advanced Counselling and Child Psychology. We provide flexible sessions, both online and in person, to support families, parents, and children in:

  • Strengthening family bonds and improving communication.
  • Providing parenting support and effective strategies.
  • Focusing on child development and emotional well-being.
  • Coping with life transitions and managing concerns such as anxiety and stress

Counselling can be helpful when you feel overwhelmed, stuck, or unsure how to manage life’s challenges on your own. You may consider counselling if you’re experiencing:

  • Emotional distress such as anxiety, depression, or anger
  • Relationship difficulties or family conflicts
  • Parenting challenges or ongoing stress
  • Major life transitions like divorce, relocation, or loss
  • Low self-esteem or a lack of confidence
  • Behavioural or emotional changes in children

You can expect a safe, confidential, and non-judgmental space where you are met with genuine empathy and respect. The counselling process includes:

  • Active listening and understanding from your counsellor
  • A personalised approach tailored to your unique needs
  • Collaborative goal-setting and clear direction
  • Learning supportive techniques and practical skills
  • Gaining self-awareness and deeper insight
  • Experiencing empowerment and growth toward a healthier, more balanced life

We provide non-clinical support for emotional well-being, including guidance for individuals, parents and children, as well as families and couples.

Session lengths may vary depending on your individual needs and the type of counselling (individual, family, or child). The exact duration will be discussed and confirmed when you schedule your appointment.
